The Time line of the life of Pope John Paul II

1938 - Wojtyła and his father left Wadowice and moved to Kraków, where he enrolled at the Jagiellonian University

1942 - , while the war continued, he knocked on the door of the Archbishop's residence in Kraków and asked to study for the priesthood. Soon after, he began courses in the clandestine underground seminary run by the Archbishop of Kraków, Cardinal Adam Stefan Sapieha.

1940 to 1944 - Wojtyła variously worked as a messenger for a restaurant, a manual labourer in a limestone quarry and for the Solvay chemical factory, in order to avoid deportation to Germany.

1946 - Wojtyła was ordained as a priest on All Saints' Day

1947 - During his sojourn at the Angelicum, Wojtyła visited Padre Pio, who heard his confession and told him that one day he would ascend to "the highest post in the Church"

1948 - Wojtyła returned to Poland in the summer for his first pastoral assignment in the village of Niegowić.

1949 -Wojtyla was transferred to the parish of Saint Florian in Kraków.

1967 - He was instrumental in formulating the encyclical Humanae vitae, which dealt with the same issues that forbid abortion and artificial birth control.

1968 -Wojtyla voted in the papal conclave, which elected John Paul I.

1978 - Wojtyla became the 264th pope according to the chronological list of popes, the first non-Italian in 455 years.

1995 - During the X World Youth Day, he offered Mass to an estimated crowd of between five and seven million in Luneta Park, Manila, Philippines, which was considered to be the largest single gathering in Christian history.

2004 - US President George W. Bush presented the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the United States' highest civilian honour, to John Paul II during a ceremony at the Apostolic Palace.

- John Paul II became the first pope known to receive a priestly blessing from a rabbi, Rabbis Benjamin Blech.
